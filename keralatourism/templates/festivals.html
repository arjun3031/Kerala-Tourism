Festivals in Kerala

Onam

Onam is the biggest festival in Kerala, celebrated with grand feasts, traditional dances, and floral decorations. It marks the homecoming of the legendary King Mahabali and is celebrated in August-September.

Vishu

Vishu is the Malayalam New Year festival, celebrated in April. It is marked by the Vishukkani (the first sight of the New Year), traditional feast, and fireworks.

Thrissur Pooram

Thrissur Pooram is one of the most famous temple festivals in Kerala, held in Thrissur. It is renowned for its grand elephant procession, fireworks, and vibrant celebrations.

Navratri

Navratri is a Hindu festival dedicated to the worship of the goddess Durga. Celebrated over nine nights, it includes dance, music, and cultural performances.

Eid

Eid is a major Islamic festival celebrated with prayers, feasting, and community gatherings. It marks the end of Ramadan and is observed with special meals and giving of charity.

Christmas

Christmas is celebrated with festive decorations, carol singing, and special church services. It commemorates the birth of Jesus Christ and is marked by joyful celebrations across Kerala.

Pongal

Pongal is a Tamil harvest festival celebrated in January. It involves cooking a special dish called Pongal and is marked by cultural events and family gatherings.

Vallamkali (Boat Race)

Vallamkali, or the boat race, is a traditional event held during Onam and other festivals. It features long snake boats racing in the backwaters of Kerala, attracting many spectators.
