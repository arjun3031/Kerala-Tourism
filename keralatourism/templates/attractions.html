Attractions in Kerala Tourism

Alleppey

Known as the "Venice of the East," Alleppey is famous for its houseboat cruises and scenic beauty. It is a perfect spot to experience the tranquil backwaters and enjoy a relaxing stay on a traditional houseboat.

Munnar

Munnar is a popular hill station in Kerala, known for its tea gardens, rolling hills, and pleasant climate. It offers breathtaking views of misty valleys and lush greenery.

Kumarakom

Kumarakom is a serene village on Vembanad Lake, renowned for its bird sanctuary, luxury resorts, and houseboat cruises. It is an ideal destination for bird watchers and nature enthusiasts.

Athirappally

Athirappally is known for its magnificent waterfall, often referred to as the "Niagara of India." The lush greenery and scenic surroundings make it a popular spot for nature lovers and photographers.

Kozhikode Beach

Kozhikode Beach is a serene and beautiful beach, perfect for enjoying sunsets and relaxing walks. The beach is also known for its seafood delicacies and historical significance.

Varkala Cliff

Varkala Cliff is a unique coastal destination with stunning cliffs overlooking the Arabian Sea. It is famous for its natural springs, yoga centers, and vibrant beach scene.

Thekkady

Thekkady is home to the Periyar National Park, a major wildlife sanctuary in Kerala. Visitors can enjoy boat safaris to spot wildlife such as elephants, tigers, and various species of birds.

Wayanad

Wayanad is known for its stunning landscapes, including waterfalls, caves, and wildlife sanctuaries. It is a great destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.

Bekal Fort

Bekal Fort, located on the coast of Kerala, is one of the largest and best-preserved forts in the state. It offers stunning views of the Arabian Sea and has historical significance dating back to the 17th century.

Palakkayam Thattu

Palakkayam Thattu is a picturesque hill station in Kerala, known for its lush greenery, cool climate, and panoramic views. It is an ideal destination for trekking, camping, and nature photography.

Fort Kochi and Jew Town

Fort Kochi and Jew Town are rich in history and culture. Fort Kochi is known for its colonial architecture, Chinese fishing nets, and vibrant arts scene, while Jew Town is famous for its antique shops, spice markets, and the historic Paradesi Synagogue.

Palakkad

Palakkad, often referred to as the "Rice Bowl of Kerala," is known for its vast paddy fields, lush green landscapes, and picturesque scenery. It is a haven for nature lovers and offers a tranquil retreat amidst its natural beauty.
